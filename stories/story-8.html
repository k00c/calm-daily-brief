Prime Minister Keir Starmer's approval ratings have dropped markedly in the weeks following his election victory, raising broader questions about the state of British governance. His initial post-election period, typically a honeymoon phase for new leaders, has instead seen growing public dissatisfaction.

Observers are debating whether the rapid decline reflects specific government actions, structural challenges facing the UK political system, or broader social conditions. Some analysts point to recent political events like Brexit as contributing factors to the current climate.

The situation underscores the difficulties facing any new government in managing public expectations and delivering on campaign promises within the complex context of modern British politics.
