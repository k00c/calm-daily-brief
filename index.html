Property Tax

Australia's Treasurer has committed to addressing an unintended consequence in the government's recent property tax overhaul, which affects widows and divorcees. The change relates to the redesign of negative gearing and property tax concessions. No specific fix has been announced, with the Treasurer indicating details will come in due course. The issue has drawn attention to the complexity of reforming long-standing property tax arrangements.

Perinatal Health

A growing network of perinatal mental health clinics offering free support is being expanded across Australia, aimed at new parents experiencing anxiety and emotional difficulty. Many parents currently manage in silence without accessing professional help. The rollout reflects increasing recognition that the period around childbirth carries significant mental health implications for both parents. Clinicians are encouraging anyone struggling to reach out to these services.

Perth Transport

The Perth-to-Bunbury train service has resumed following an extended period of breakdowns and operational delays. The resumption marks a significant step for regional connectivity between Perth and the South West of Western Australia. Local residents have welcomed the return of the service, though some remain cautious given its troubled recent history. Operators say improvements have been made to address the reliability issues that plagued earlier operations.

NZ Policy

A New Zealand parliamentary committee has recommended that a bill to make English an official language proceed to a vote without amendment. The proposal has attracted substantial public opposition, including from Māori and multicultural communities concerned about the symbolic and practical implications. The Justice Select Committee's recommendation means the bill moves forward in its current form. The legislation would formally enshrine English alongside existing official languages.

Bali Environment

Bali is facing a growing waste management challenge as large volumes of tourist-generated rubbish strain the island's infrastructure. Residents and local observers note that limited public education around waste disposal and the partial closure of a major landfill have compounded the problem. The island, long celebrated for its natural and cultural beauty, is under increasing pressure to find sustainable solutions. Community voices are calling for coordinated action from both government and the tourism industry.

AI & Education

Some Australian teachers are returning to traditional classroom methods in response to widespread AI use among students, with many young people saying they find it difficult to study without AI tools. Educators and researchers are raising questions about the long-term impact on foundational learning skills such as writing, reasoning, and independent research. The shift is prompting schools to reconsider how they design assessments and classroom activities. The trend is being observed across multiple year levels and subject areas.

Australia Social Media Long read

Moment that mattered: Australia's under-16 social media ban comes into effect

On 10th December, Australia became the first country in the world to ban children from social media. In theory, at least, anyone aged under 16 could no longer use platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, TikTok and X. When the Australian parliament first approved the ban in November 2024, prime minister Anthony Albanese said the legislation would make social media companies responsible for keeping children safe online.
